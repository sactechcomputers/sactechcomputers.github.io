Who We Are

The Sir Ahmadu Bello Memorial Foundation (SABMF) was founded by the Governors of the 19 northern states of Nigeria in cooperation with the associates, friends, admirers and well-wishers of the late Sir Ahmadu Bello, KBE, the Sardauna of Sokoto and Premier of the defunct northern region

The Sir Ahmadu Bello Memorial Foundation was established to pursue the legacy of leadership and good governance bequeathed by late Sir Ahmadu Bello to bring Northern Nigeria back to reckoning in terms of human and economic development. It was launched in 2009.

The Foundation is a hybrid grant making/receiving, not-for-profit and non-governmental organization, and was registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission on 19th January, 2009.

SABMF is conceived as an intervention agency that conceptualizes, designs, develops and executes programmes directly, partnering with other global and local NGOs or by collaborating with special interests that share the same or similar objectives with us in realizing their set goals and objectives which should be within the general framework for socio-economic development.

The Foundation mandate is geared towards fostering the legacy of exemplary leadership and good governance bequeathed by Sir Ahmadu Bello for sustainable human and economic development of the 19 northern State and the FCT. Its office is located in Kaduna, Kaduna State, in the North West geopolitical zone of Nigeria.

Aim and Objectives

Aim

To carry out research and on the life and legacies of the late Sir Ahmadu Bello, the Sardauna of Sokoto and Premier of the Northern Region, particularly in the areas of values in service, Leadership, Development Strategies, Education, Religious and Ethnic Tolerance, Transparency and Accountability towards immortalizing the late icon.

Objectives

  • To conduct Studies and Research on how the Legacies of the Late Sir Ahmadu Bello can be inculcated in the present youth and for generation yet unborn.
  • To raise money for the purpose of pursuing the aims and objectives of the Foundation.
  • To organize seminars, conferences, workshops, and other capacity building programmes in order to achieve the goals and objectives of the Foundation.
  • To seeking and encouraging support for the Foundation in any way necessary.
  • To encourage and facilitate the provision of voluntary services by professionals, communities and associations.
  • To develop and maintain communication and collaboration with all persons, national and international agencies and bodies who have similar interests, aims and objectives as the Foundation.
  • To promote the ideals of development and peaceful coexistence practiced by Sir Ahmadu Bello, the Sardauna of Sokoto through:
  • - Strengthening of bonds of mutual understanding; - The promotion of good governance; - Lending support to conflict management and resolution
  • To promote human rights as enunciated in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (adopted and proclaimed by the General Assembly of the United Nations Resolution 217 of 10 December 1948) and the African Charter on Human and Peoples Rights (as ratified by Nigeria), for the good of the public by: - Furthering education in human rights (through seminars, workshops, teaching, conducting research, etc.); - Increasing awareness of human rights through publications and the media, provided that this does not conflict with established rules and regulations; - Promoting skills acquisition and development for the youth; - Promoting child welfare and the economic development of women; - Providing needy communities with basic amenities
  • To help address the question of environmental sanity and sustainability by: - Conducting or commissioning research and publishing the results of such research in this area; - Promoting a safe and healthy environment;
  • To develop a permanent institutional capacity for the attainment of the aim and objectives of the Foundation.
  • To help alleviate poverty by: - Promoting skills acquisition and development for the youth - Promoting child welfare and the economic development of women - Providing needy communities with basic amenities
